About Me

I am a multidisciplinary artist and technologist working at the intersection of physical form, digital systems, and human perception. With a background in photography, printmaking, and creative coding, I build projects that move between material and computational spaces, exploring how pattern, control, and disruption shape the way we see and interpret the world. My work investigates how perception is structured: how repetition creates meaning, how systems influence attention, and how digital processes alter our sense of authorship and reality. Across mediums, I examine the tension between order and chaos to question what feels stable, constructed, or true.
